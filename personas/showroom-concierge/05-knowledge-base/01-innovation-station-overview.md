## 1. What is the Alstom Innovation Station?

The Alstom Innovation Station is the **regional innovation hub for the APAC region**, located at Alstom's APAC Regional Head Office in **Singapore**. It is both a **physical showroom** and an **innovation programme** that anticipates global shifts in mobility, transforms challenges into opportunities, and showcases Alstom's vision for the future of rail to clients, partners, employees, and visitors.

The Station's mission can be summarised as: *to lead the way to greener and smarter mobility worldwide, supported by demographic growth, rising urbanisation, and increasing environmental concerns.* As a creative pioneer in rail mobility, Alstom looks at things differently — constantly adapting and applying a century of embedded engineering knowledge to new solutions for the future.

The rail industry is more than just trains and tracks: it serves the noble cause of **decarbonising transportation and positively impacting society**. The Innovation Station is the physical and intellectual space where this purpose is made tangible.

## 3. The four booths of the Innovation Station

The Innovation Station's content is organised into **four physical booths**:

### Booth 1 — Green Mobility
Sustainability, energy savings, eco-design, lifecycle management, end-of-life recovery. Focus on tropical and APAC-specific environmental challenges.

### Booth 2 — Smart Mobility
AI, robotics, computer vision, IoT, autonomous systems applied to rolling stock and infrastructure. Cross-cutting pillar.

### Booth 3 — Immersive Mobility
Virtual and augmented reality (VR/AR) for training, remote assistance, tender support, and immersive client engagement. Built primarily on the Uptale platform.

### Booth 4 — 3D Printing (Additive Manufacturing)
On-demand local manufacturing for spare parts, obsolescence solving, prototyping, and rapid turnaround. Supported by Alstom's 3DPaaS service model.

## 6. Alstom's corporate innovation pillars (broader context)

While the Innovation Station has four booths, the broader **Alstom corporate innovation strategy** is organised around four interconnected pillars. Kai can mention these if a visitor asks "how does the Innovation Station fit into Alstom's wider innovation strategy?":

1. **Smarter rail** — digitalisation, AI, automation, data-driven tools (predictive maintenance, autonomous trains, CBTC signalling).
2. **Greener mobility** — net zero across the full rail lifecycle (clean propulsion, eco-design, lightweight and recycled materials).
3. **Embedding resilience** — designing for shocks (environmental, geopolitical, digital). Examples: rolling stock that runs at -40°C in Canada or +58°C in Riyadh.
4. **Designing experiences** — accessible, modular, adaptable rail co-created with passengers and communities. Alstom has the industry's only **in-house Passenger Experience department**, with 550 dedicated experts.

The Innovation Station's four booths roughly map to these pillars: Green Mobility ↔ Greener mobility; Smart Mobility ↔ Smarter rail; Immersive Mobility ↔ Designing experiences (also supporting Smarter rail); 3D Printing ↔ Embedding resilience (supply chain resilience) and Greener mobility (material efficiency).

## 7. What visitors typically come to see or discuss

Common visitor profiles and what they tend to be most interested in:

- **Government officials and regulators** → Green Mobility (sustainability commitments), Immersive Mobility (training & safety).
- **Public transport operators** → Smart Mobility (operational efficiency), 3D Printing (spare parts and obsolescence), OTES specifically.
- **Journalists / media** → flagship "wow" projects: Coil Nucleus story, 3DPaaS global numbers, EHS VR adoption.
- **Internal Alstom colleagues from other regions** → looking for transferable projects, methodology, success stories to replicate.
- **Universities and startups** → collaboration opportunities, especially Smart Mobility (Computer Vision, GenAI).
- **VIP corporate visitors** → broad showroom tour, with emphasis on tangible artefacts (3D printed parts, VR headset demo).

## 8. Tone and editorial line

The Innovation Station communicates with a **confident, factual, solution-oriented voice**. We highlight:

- Tangible results and KPIs (energy %, lead time reductions, ROI)
- Local relevance (Singapore, APAC, tropical conditions)
- Customer partnerships and co-creation (the Innovation Station does not "sell to" customers — it **works with** them)
- The link between innovation and Alstom's mission of decarbonising mobility

We avoid:

- Vague buzzwords without evidence
- Competitive comparisons that could be construed as criticism of other OEMs
- Promises of timelines or commercial terms
- Confidential project information
