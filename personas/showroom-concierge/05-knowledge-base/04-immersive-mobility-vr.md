## 1. Pillar mission

**Immersive Mobility** refers to the use of **virtual and augmented reality (VR/AR)** within Alstom. These tools serve three main purposes:

1. **Training** — for employees, technicians, and customer site workers
2. **Remote assistance and operations** — for distributed teams and trackside work
3. **Tender and client engagement support** — making complex rail solutions tangible during commercial discussions

The Immersive Mobility booth at the Innovation Station is one of the most **hands-on** parts of the showroom — visitors can actually wear a VR headset and experience training modules, density simulations, and EHS scenarios first-hand.

## 2. Technology stack

- **Platform**: **Uptale** — the primary authoring and deployment platform used across Alstom's VR projects
- **Hardware**: Meta Quest VR headsets (6 units deployed in the Malaysia ICP project alone), 360° cameras for content capture
- **Integration**: VR content connects to Alstom's LMS (Learning Management System) for tracking and certification
- **Compatibility**: VR experiences also run on standard desktop browsers — visitors don't need a headset to access training

## 3. Flagship project — EHS Induction in VR (Singapore BPLRT)

### What it is
A **mandatory Environment, Health & Safety (EHS) induction** delivered entirely through Virtual Reality at the **BPLRT (Bukit Panjang LRT)** depot in Singapore.

### The challenge
Traditional EHS induction sessions face two big challenges:
- **Limited trainer availability** — depots can't always wait for a qualified trainer to come on-site
- **Language barriers** — depot staff often speak multiple languages, and a single trainer can't cover all of them

This leads to inconsistent safety communication and delays in onboarding new staff, contractors, and visitors.

### The solution
Using the **Uptale platform**, Alstom developed an EHS induction programme based on **360° images and videos** of the actual depot environment. The experience is interactive — learners navigate the depot virtually, with quizzes embedded at key safety points to reinforce understanding.

### Key features
- **Available in all languages** — maximum understanding for a global workforce
- **Everywhere and anytime** — runs on both desktop and VR headsets
- **Immersive** — 360° images and videos of the actual depot for realistic safety visualisation
- **Interactive learning** — embedded quizzes reinforce understanding
- **Certification at the end** — ensures compliance and training validation

### Achieved results
- **Deployed in Singapore at BPLRT**
- **Mandatory for all depot visitors**
- **310 learners trained between February 2024 and November 2025**
- **ROI achieved in 1 year**

### Why this matters
EHS induction is one of those use cases where VR doesn't just modernise an existing process — it **removes blockers**. New contractors can complete induction the moment they arrive on-site. Visiting teams from other regions can do it before they even land. Language barriers disappear. And every learner gets exactly the same content, with no drift due to trainer style or fatigue.

### Talking point for Kai
"The EHS VR induction at our BPLRT depot is a great example of immersive tech solving a real operational problem. Over 300 people trained, 1-year ROI, and now mandatory for every visitor — that's not a flashy demo, that's a process improvement."

## 4. Flagship project — VR Training for Malaysia ICP (Prasarana)

### What it is
A **complete VR training package** delivered by Alstom to **Prasarana** (the Malaysian public transport operator), as part of Alstom Malaysia's **ICP (Industrial Collaboration Programme)** obligation under a government contract.

### Background — what is ICP?
The **Industrial Collaboration Programme** is a Malaysian government **procurement scheme**, administered by **TDA (Technology Depository Agency of the Malaysian government)**. Foreign OEMs (Original Equipment Manufacturers) that win Malaysian government contracts are required to **contribute to the local economy** through specific ICP commitments — technology transfer, training, local sourcing, etc.

In Alstom Malaysia's case, the ICP obligation was fulfilled through a structured VR training programme delivered to Prasarana.

### Scope of work delivered by the Innovation Station

1. **Support for the first VR experience creation** — from concept to deployment
2. **Support on Uptale access transition** to end-users for Year 2, conditional on user satisfaction
3. **Arrangement of a 1-week user training** (one-off) and **delivery of hardware** (outsourced to Uptale)

### Deliverables (package)

| # | Item | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Uptale subscription | 1 year, 15 admins, 1,500 learners |
| 2 | VR Experience | 1 custom-built |
| 3 | VR headsets | 6 units |
| 4 | 360° camera | 1 unit |
| 5 | LMS integration | Yes |

### Project phasing
The project ran across four phases from April through December:

- **Phase 1 — Contract & Platform Creation**: Uptale contract, legal agreement, platform access setup, first experience scoping
- **Phase 2 — Experience Creation**: Content collection, training, experience building, testing/feedback iterations between Alstom and the client
- **Phase 3 — Support Period**: User support and account management through end of Year 1
- **Phase 4 — Handover**: Survey, GO/NO-GO decision for extension, migration contract handover

### Why this case matters
The Malaysia ICP project is an **example of how the Innovation Station supports business obligations** beyond pure innovation — it can deliver tangible commitments that help Alstom win and retain government contracts in APAC.

## 5. Flagship project — Changi APM Tender (Train Density Simulator)

### What it is
A **VR experience** that lets clients and planners **feel** what different passenger densities look and feel like inside a train, **before** the commercial decision is made on train sizing and timetable.

### The context
As part of the **pre-tender phase** for the **Changi APM (Automated People Mover)** project at Singapore Changi Airport, Alstom needed to determine:

- The **maximum acceptable passenger density** inside the trains
- The **required number of trains** to meet that density
- The **dwell time** at each station (how long doors stay open)

These are not just engineering questions — they are also **comfort and customer experience** questions. A train that's technically full at 6 passengers/m² is also potentially uncomfortable for passengers.

### The solution
A VR experience that **simulates a real-life rail car** filled with a variable number of virtual passengers per square meter. The user wearing the headset can physically look around, walk through (within the VR boundary), and **feel** the difference between 4, 5, 6, and 7 passengers per square meter.

### Highlights

- **Decision Support** — helps clients and planners better understand comfort levels and capacity constraints before finalising train sizing.
- **Interactive Scenarios** — different density levels and configurations can be toggled to assess operational feasibility.
- **Enhanced Engagement** — facilitates clearer communication with non-technical stakeholders (e.g., government officials, airport executives) through realistic visual representation.

### Achieved result
The client (Changi Airport project team) was **very satisfied** and was able to visualise and determine the maximum density — making the dwell time and fleet sizing decision much easier.

### Why this matters
This is a great example of VR being used **commercially**, not just for training. It turns abstract numbers ("6 passengers per square meter") into a felt experience, which dramatically improves the quality of stakeholder decisions.

## 6. Flagship project — VR Training for HK Site (Cable Pulling, T&C, EHS)

### What it is
A **suite of three VR training modules** developed for Alstom's **Hong Kong site**, covering three high-risk activities.

### The three modules

#### 1. VR Cable Pulling Training
Simulates **cable installation steps and safety checks** in a virtual environment. Cable pulling is a physically demanding and high-risk task — VR lets engineers practise the sequence and safety procedures before doing it on a live site.

#### 2. VR Test & Commissioning (T&C) Training
Covers **trackside, SER (Signalling Equipment Room), and trainborne** commissioning procedures in an interactive way. T&C is the phase when a new rail system is tested and certified before going into service — getting it right is critical.

#### 3. VR EHS Training
Focuses on **safe access and hazard awareness** for depot, station, and trackside work.

### Why these modules exist
These activities are exactly the kind of work where you cannot afford on-site mistakes:

- Cable pulling errors can damage expensive infrastructure
- T&C mistakes can delay project go-live by weeks
- EHS lapses can injure or kill people

### Benefits

- **Reduces mistakes** — new engineers practise site work in a safe office environment before going on-site
- **Saves time for senior staff** — no need to repeat the same explanations to every new joiner
- **Increases safety awareness** — shows what to watch out for at every step
- **Provides a certificate** at the end of training, ensuring compliance

### Talking point for Kai
"The VR modules for our Hong Kong site are a good answer to a real challenge — how do you train a new engineer on cable pulling, commissioning, and depot safety without putting them or the infrastructure at risk on day one? You let them practise in VR first."

## 8. Common visitor questions on Immersive Mobility

**Q: Can I try the VR?**
A: Absolutely — that's exactly what the Immersive Mobility booth is for. There's a Meta Quest headset on-site and a few short demos to choose from — EHS induction, train density simulator, or the cable pulling module. Pick one, put it on, and dive in.

**Q: What platform do you use?**
A: Uptale is our main authoring platform. It runs on both VR headsets and desktop browsers, integrates with our LMS, and supports content in any language.

**Q: How long does it take to build a VR module?**
A: Depending on complexity, anywhere from a few weeks for a 360° image-based experience to a few months for a fully interactive 3D module. The first version of the Malaysia ICP training took us about 3 phases over several months from contract signing to first delivery.

**Q: Is VR replacing live training?**
A: It's complementing it, not replacing it. VR is fantastic for repetitive scenarios, language-flexible content, and safe practice of high-risk procedures. Live training is still essential for hands-on tasks and team coordination.

**Q: What's the ROI on VR training?**
A: For our BPLRT EHS induction in Singapore, we achieved ROI within 1 year — over 300 learners trained with consistent quality, no language barriers, and no need to schedule a trainer for each session.

**Q: What about AR? Augmented Reality?**
A: AR is part of the broader Immersive Mobility scope and is mentioned across Alstom for things like AR-assisted maintenance. The booth focuses primarily on VR today, but AR projects are being explored — happy to share more if you'd like to talk to our team.
