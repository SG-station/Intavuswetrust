## 1. Pillar mission

Alstom's Green Mobility pillar is dedicated to the **commitment of taking up the challenge of sustainable mobility**. With rapidly evolving demographics, rising urbanisation, and growing demand for transport, rail systems must be redesigned to meet that demand while **protecting the environment** and improving the everyday travel experience for passengers.

The Green Mobility booth at the Innovation Station showcases concrete projects that pursue three goals:

- **Reduce environmental impact** of train operations, manufacturing, and end-of-life.
- **Improve energy efficiency** across the entire rail system.
- **Provide audited, transparent sustainability data** that customers and passengers can trust.

## 2. Flagship project — OTES (Optimized Timetable for Energy Savings)

### What it is
A **smart, software-based solution** using advanced algorithms to **synchronise accelerating and braking trains** in a metro network, so that the energy generated by one train's regenerative braking is immediately reused by another train that is accelerating.

### Why it matters
In metro systems, regenerative braking converts kinetic energy back into electricity during deceleration. But much of this electricity is **lost** if there is no other train ready to consume it immediately. OTES solves this by **optimising the timetable** — without compromising operational constraints and without requiring any additional hardware investment.

### Achieved results
- **Up to 95% braking energy recovery**
- **Up to 15% traction energy savings**
- Easy implementation (software only)
- Respects operational constraints
- Delivers direct, measurable energy and cost benefits

### Roadmap and partners
- **2023–2024 — Trial phase**: trials conducted with **SBST (SBS Transit)** and **SMRT** in Singapore. Solution refined based on real operational feedback (REX).
- **2025 — Implementation**: contract signed between **Alstom and SBST for the NEL (North-East Line)**.
- **2026 — Development**: continued improvement, extension to further lines, exploration of new business models (e.g., performance-based contracts, carbon-credit-linked pricing).

### Talking point for Kai
"OTES is a great example of how a pure software innovation can deliver up to 15% energy savings on a metro network — no hardware change, no operational compromise. It's the kind of upgrade an operator can deploy quickly and see results on the next billing cycle."

## 3. Flagship project — Trains' Next Life

### What it is
A **local turnkey service** to manage the **end of life of trains**, designed to maximise sustainability and minimise carbon footprint. The service is provided by Alstom in partnership with **Nandina REM**, a specialist in railway end-of-life solutions.

### The challenge
Managing the end of life of trains is technically and logistically complex. When done badly, it generates waste and lost value. When done well, it can drastically reduce the environmental impact of the rail asset over its full lifecycle.

### How it works (process flow)
1. **Asset owner** has an end-of-life train.
2. **Alstom manages** the handover, end-to-end.
3. The train goes to a **Dismantler**.
4. Materials and components are routed to **Recyclers / End-of-life actors**.
5. Throughout the process, Alstom provides **environmental impact calculation and reporting** back to the asset owner.

### Why customers want this
- **Hassle-free** — Alstom handles the full chain
- **Transparent** — they receive an end-of-life management report including carbon footprint, suitable for ESG reporting and traceability
- **Virtuous** — sustainability is maximised, and the local supply chain grows
- **Compliant** with local regulations

### Status
- 2025 — Pre-collaboration with Nandina REM set up
- 2026 — Target first pilot in collaborative mode
- 2028 — Scale-up at company level, offered as a service to all asset owners

## 4. Flagship project — HVAC Eco-Coating

### What it is
A **thin, conformal graphene-based coating** applied to aluminium fins and tube interfaces on train **HVAC heat exchangers**.

### The problem it solves
In tropical environments (Singapore, Malaysia, coastal Middle East), aluminium HVAC fins suffer **rapid corrosion**, **pitting**, and **fin-loss** due to:
- High humidity
- Airborne chlorides (saline air)
- Industrial pollutants

Corroded fins reduce airflow and heat-transfer efficiency, which increases compressor load and energy consumption. Over time, this leads to **premature HVAC unit replacement** — often after only 5–8 years in tropical zones instead of the expected 15–20.

### How the coating works
- Acts as a **corrosion barrier** against chloride attack, reducing pitting initiation and growth
- Provides a **hydrophobic surface** that reduces salt and dust adhesion and fouling
- Maintains (and slightly improves) **thermal conductivity** at the fin surface, preserving heat-transfer performance

### Results (corrosion testing)
- Tested up to **3,000 hours of accelerated corrosion**
- 3,000 hours ≈ **20 years of real-world exposure** in tropical conditions
- Graphene-coated aluminium only starts degrading after **20 years**
- HEX efficiency stays consistent for the first **15 years**

### Benefits summary
- **Technical**: reduced pitting and fin-loss, lower fouling, maintained thermal efficiency
- **Operational/Cost**: longer coil life, lower replacement cost, less frequent maintenance, lower energy use
- **Strategic**: competitive differentiator in tropical and coastal tenders

### Talking point for Kai
"The HVAC Eco-Coating is one of those innovations that's invisible to the passenger but transformative for the operator — it can extend the life of an HVAC unit from 8 years to over 20 in a tropical climate like Singapore."

## 6. Flagship project — Battery Useful Life Analytics

### What it is
A **smart battery health system** that combines AI and physics-based modelling to predict battery failures and optimise battery usage in rolling stock.

### Why it matters
- **Unexpected battery failures** cause train immobilisation, delays, safety risks, and contractual penalties.
- Current battery health models often **overestimate lifespan** — leading to nasty surprises.
- The **reputation** of batteries (e.g., risk of fire, premature degradation) is a **major obstacle** to the adoption of green mobility solutions like BEMUs (Battery Electric Multiple Units).

### How it works
- **AI-based, physics-informed model** collects and analyses rolling stock battery data.
- Assesses **state of health** dynamically.
- Predicts failures **before they happen**.
- Provides **guidance for battery usage optimisation** based on actual operational conditions and environment.

### Benefits
- **Prediction of battery failure** before it occurs
- **Expanded lifespan** through better usage patterns
- **Improved battery sizing** for new projects

### Current status
- Submitted to the Alstom Innovation Kiosk
- A similar model has already shown good results on **CVCM (Condition Validation and Conditioning Module)** and **Track Circuit** systems
- Applied use cases: **Wellington BEMU** and **NGT Trams**

### Why Singapore is the right place to develop this
- Local skilled data team
- Partnership with **A*STAR ARTC** for knowledge transfer
- Strong demand from Australian and New Zealand rail projects

## 7. Flagship project — Alstom Green Claims

### What it is
A **communication tool** designed to make Alstom's eco-design achievements visible to passengers, customers, and the public.

### The insight

### Key facts to know

- **Newly developed solutions contain 25% recycled content on average**
- **The floors of the new generation Alstom Coradia Stream regional trains contain recycled PET, made from waste plastic bottles**

### Benefits

- **Fosters best practices** — highlights best-in-class solutions, drives the sector
- **Increases credibility and desirability** — measurable data and audits foster passenger trust

## 8. Flagship project — Energy Consumption Advisory

### What it is
A **holistic service** to help operators reach their energy savings goals — combining hardware, dashboards, optimisation, and a profit-sharing model linked to **carbon credits**.

### The context
Operators have access to lots of data but **limited validation** of what energy savings are actually delivered. This slows down adoption and customer recognition of Alstom's energy-saving solutions.

### Service structure (process)
1. **Hardware installation** — covered by Alstom
2. **Dashboard creation and analysis** — covered by Alstom
3. **Optimisation** — paid by the customer
4. **Savings validation** — profit-sharing model

The flow generates **carbon credits**, which form part of the value returned to the operator.

### Benefits for each party

**For Alstom:**
- Access to operational data
- Profit-sharing revenue

**For the operator:**
- **No initial investment**
- **Cashback in carbon credits**

### Existing bricks (already available)
- Hardware
- Energy consumption dashboards
- Analytics capability
- Energy savings solutions

### To be built
- Profit-sharing commercial model
- Advisory contract framework
- Support for setting up carbon credit certification

## 10. Common visitor questions on Green Mobility

**Q: How much energy can a metro actually save?**
A: With OTES, up to 15% traction energy savings — without any hardware investment.

**Q: What's so different about rail vs other transport?**
A: Per passenger-kilometre, rail is one of the most efficient transport modes. But the sector still has emissions to cut — Alstom's commitment is net-zero across the full lifecycle: clean propulsion, eco-design, lightweight and recycled materials.

**Q: What happens to a train at end of life?**
A: Today it depends a lot on the operator. With Trains' Next Life, Alstom handles the full chain — dismantling, recycling, reporting — and the operator gets an audited carbon footprint report.

**Q: Are these solutions only for new trains?**
A: No — many can be retrofitted. OTES is software-only. HVAC Eco-Coating can be applied during maintenance. The Energy Consumption Advisory works on existing fleets.

**Q: Is Singapore important for Green Mobility?**
A: Yes — tropical climate makes HVAC Eco-Coating uniquely relevant. Singapore is also where OTES is being deployed (NEL), where battery analytics is being developed (A*STAR), and where the Mobility Collage workshop has run with LTA.
