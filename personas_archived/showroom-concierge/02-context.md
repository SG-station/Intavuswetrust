## The Innovation Station — what and why

The Alstom Innovation Station is the **regional innovation hub for the APAC region**, based at Alstom's APAC Regional Head Office in **Singapore**. It exists to anticipate global shifts in mobility, transform challenges into opportunities, and showcase Alstom's vision for the future of rail to clients, partners, and employees.

Alstom's mission is to **lead the way to greener and smarter mobility worldwide**. The rail sector serves the noble cause of decarbonising transportation while shaping more connected, resilient, and inclusive cities. The Innovation Station is where that vision becomes tangible.

The Station is structured around **four booths**, each representing a strategic pillar of innovation:

## Booth 1 — Green Mobility

**One-line pitch:** Designing rail solutions that minimise environmental impact across the full lifecycle.

**Flagship projects to remember:**
- **OTES (Optimized Timetable for Energy Savings)** — software that synchronises accelerating and braking trains in metro systems to maximise reuse of braking energy. Up to **95% braking energy recovery**, up to **15% traction energy savings**. Trialled with **SBST and SMRT** in 2023-2024, now contracted for Singapore's **NEL (North-East Line)**.
- **HVAC Eco-Coating** — a graphene-based coating for train HVAC heat exchangers. Tested to 3,000 hours of corrosion (equivalent to **20 years** in tropical/coastal conditions). Strong competitive edge for tenders in Southeast Asia and the Middle East.
- **Battery Useful Life Analytics** — an AI + physics model that predicts battery failures and optimises usage. Partnership with **A*STAR ARTC**. Applied to Wellington BEMU and NGT Trams.
- **Trains' Next Life** — turnkey end-of-life management with partner **Nandina REM**. First pilot in 2026.
- **Alstom Green Claims** — communication tool with audited eco-design data. Fact to know: **100% of Alstom's newly developed solutions are eco-designed**, and they contain **25% recycled content on average**. The Coradia Stream regional train has floors made from recycled PET (waste plastic bottles).
- **Sustainability Awareness workshops** — mobility collage sessions done with **LTA Singapore**, India and Australia.

## Booth 2 — Smart Mobility

**One-line pitch:** Applying AI, robotics, IoT and computer vision to make rail safer, faster, and more appealing.

**Flagship projects to remember:**
- **Robot Underframe Inspection** — a tracked robot that inspects train undersides remotely after a strike (rail collision). Solves a real operational problem: **70% of strikes in Queensland do not require the train to return to depot immediately** — remote inspection keeps trains in service.
- **Computer vision, generative AI, IoT and AR** applied to multiple sub-systems across Alstom: predictive maintenance, robotic train building, digital twins.

Smart Mobility is the most cross-cutting booth — many projects in this pillar are still in active development, and collaborations with universities, start-ups, suppliers and customers are constantly expanding the portfolio.

## Booth 3 — Immersive Mobility (Virtual Reality)

**One-line pitch:** Virtual and augmented reality for training, remote assistance, and immersive client engagement.

**Flagship projects to remember:**
- **EHS Induction in VR** — deployed at Singapore BPLRT depot. **Mandatory for all depot visitors. 310 learners between February 2024 and November 2025. ROI achieved in 1 year.** Built on the Uptale platform.
- **VR Training for Malaysia ICP (Prasarana)** — delivered to fulfil Alstom Malaysia's ICP (Industrial Collaboration Programme) obligation. Package: Uptale subscription (15 admins, 1,500 learners), 6 VR headsets, 1 × 360° camera, LMS integration.
- **Changi APM Tender — Train Density Simulator** — a VR experience that lets clients physically *feel* what passenger density looks like at different levels per square meter. Used pre-tender to size fleet and dwell time.
- **VR for HK Site** — three modules: Cable Pulling, Test & Commissioning (T&C), and EHS for trackside/depot/station work.

Tech stack: **Uptale platform**, Meta Quest headsets, 360° cameras for content capture.

## Booth 4 — 3D Printing (Additive Manufacturing)

**One-line pitch:** Rapid, on-demand, local manufacturing to solve obsolescence and speed up the supply chain.

**Flagship projects to remember:**
- **3D Printing as a Service (3DPaaS)** — a distributed network of 3D printing hubs. **Global results: 170,000+ parts printed, €16M+ in cost savings, 100,000+ days saved, 400+ tonnes of CO₂ avoided.** APAC alone: **7,613 parts in 2 years, 4 hubs created** (Singapore, Bangkok, Wellington, Dongguan).
- **Current Collector Shoe Reload** — extends the lifecycle of collector shoes using Direct Ink Writing (DIW) for carbon inserts and Plasma Spray for copper. Reduces waste, lowers cost, shortens lead times.
- **Coil Nucleus for SBS Transit** — **Alstom Singapore's first-ever commercial 3D-printed spare part order (250 pieces)**. Performance **doubled** (40,000 cycles vs 17,000), **6× faster lead time** (2-5 weeks instead of 28), and **45% cheaper to manufacture**.
- **Thailand cable clamp** (anecdote-worthy story): an obsolete cable clamp from the OEM had a 24-week lead time. Alstom reverse-engineered and 3D-printed a validated part **in 5 days**, keeping service running.
- **Cable Clamp Monorail** — 83% cost savings, lead time reduced by 20+ days. DLP and SLS resin printing.
- **TGV Dumper in 316L stainless steel** — metal printing for durability, lead time reduced by 60+ days.

Standards & scale: Alstom uses 18 special AM processes, EN45545 rail-compliant materials (polymers, composites, metals), and has **30+ AM experts deployed globally**.
